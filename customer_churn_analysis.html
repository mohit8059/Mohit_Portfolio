Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard

An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze customer churn behavior, identify high-risk customer segments, and discover the main reasons behind customer loss.

View GitHub Back to Portfolio

Project Overview

The Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard is created to understand why customers leave a service and which customer groups are more likely to churn.

This project analyzes customer behavior based on contract type, payment method, tenure, monthly charges, internet service, senior citizen status and support services. The dashboard helps businesses identify weak areas and improve customer retention.

This project helped me practice Power BI dashboard design, DAX measures, Power Query data cleaning, KPI creation, customer segmentation and business insight generation.

Business Problem

Problem Statement

A telecom company is facing customer churn. The management wants to understand which customers are leaving, what factors are causing churn, and which customer segments should be targeted for retention campaigns.

Main Objective

The objective of this dashboard is to track churn rate, analyze customer behavior, identify high-risk customers, and provide actionable insights for reducing churn.

Important DAX Measures

Total Customers = DISTINCTCOUNT(Customer_Churn[CustomerID])

Churned Customers =
CALCULATE(
    [Total Customers],
    Customer_Churn[Churn] = "Yes"
)

Active Customers =
CALCULATE(
    [Total Customers],
    Customer_Churn[Churn] = "No"
)

Churn Rate % =
DIVIDE(
    [Churned Customers],
    [Total Customers],
    0
)

Average Monthly Charges = AVERAGE(Customer_Churn[MonthlyCharges])

Average Tenure = AVERAGE(Customer_Churn[Tenure])

Calculated Column Used

Tenure Group =
SWITCH(
    TRUE(),
    Customer_Churn[Tenure] <= 12, "0-12 Months",
    Customer_Churn[Tenure] <= 24, "13-24 Months",
    Customer_Churn[Tenure] <= 48, "25-48 Months",
    "49+ Months"
)

Key Insights

1. Month-to-Month Contract Risk

Customers using month-to-month contracts showed higher churn compared to customers with one-year or two-year contracts.

2. Low Tenure Customers

Customers with lower tenure were more likely to leave the service, which shows that early customer engagement is important.

3. High Monthly Charges

Customers with higher monthly charges showed stronger churn behavior, indicating possible pricing dissatisfaction.

4. Electronic Check Payment

Electronic check payment method had a higher churn rate compared to automatic payment methods.

5. Fiber Optic Customers

Fiber optic internet users showed higher churn, which may indicate service quality or pricing related issues.

6. Support Services Impact

Customers without online security or tech support were more likely to churn, showing the importance of support services.
